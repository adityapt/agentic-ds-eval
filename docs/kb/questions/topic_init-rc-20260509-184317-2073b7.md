# Stage 01: Topic Init

# SMART Research Goal: Multi-Agent Multi-Base-Model Empirical Evaluation of Agentic AI in Product and Marketing Data Science

---

## Topic

**Agentic AI for Product & Marketing Data Science** — systematic empirical evaluation of autonomous AI agents across the full data-science lifecycle on industry-representative tasks spanning marketing attribution, customer behavior modeling, recommendation, and retail forecasting.

---

## Novel Angle

### What has NOT been well-studied?

Existing agentic-AI benchmarks (MLE-bench, DS-1000, SWE-bench, AIDE leaderboard, AutoML-Bench) share three structural blind spots that this work closes simultaneously:

| Blind Spot | Existing Work | This Work |
|---|---|---|
| **Agent × Model factorization** | Evaluations couple agent scaffold to a single proprietary LLM (e.g., GPT-4o in AutoGen demos) | Full 3×3 factorial design isolates scaffold variance from model variance |
| **Domain specificity** | Generic ML tasks (Iris, titanic, HuggingFace datasets) or software engineering | Six tasks drawn from *marketing and product* data science with domain-specific KPIs (ROAS, WRMSSE, MAP@12) |
| **Lifecycle × Autonomy granularity** | Binary pass/fail or single aggregate score | 7-stage lifecycle × 5-level autonomy rubric (SAE-inspired L1–L5) per agent-model cell |
| **Open vs. closed base-model parity** | Closed-model agents dominate leaderboards; open models rarely evaluated in agentic settings | DeepSeek-V3 and Qwen2.5-Coder-32B-Instruct compared on equal footing with Claude 4.6 Sonnet |
| **Attribution-heavy task hardness** | No prior work distinguishes routine predictive tasks from causal/attribution tasks for autonomy ceiling analysis | H2 explicitly tests autonomy-ceiling asymmetry between MMM/Forecasting vs. Churn/Coupon/Recommendation |

### Why is this timely NOW (2024–2026)?

Three converging developments create the opportunity window:

1. **Agentic scaffolds matured to production-readiness (2024):** AIDE (Weco AI, 2024) achieved top-16% on Kaggle benchmarks autonomously; AutoGen 0.4 (Microsoft, 2024) introduced a fully async actor-model architecture enabling long-horizon tasks; Aider crossed 60%+ on SWE-bench-verified. These tools are stable enough for controlled comparison but have never been co-evaluated on the same task suite.

2. **Open frontier models closed the capability gap (late 2024):** DeepSeek-V3 (671B MoE, Dec 2024) matched GPT-4o on coding benchmarks; Qwen2.5-Coder-32B-Instruct set open-source SOTA on HumanEval and DS-1000. The practical question of whether open models are viable drop-in agent backends for enterprise data science is unanswered.

3. **Marketing-tech AI adoption pressure:** The deprecation of third-party cookies (Chrome, 2024 rollout), the rise of incrementality testing, and the proliferation of MMM toolkits (Robyn, PyMC-Marketing, LightweightMMM) have made automated marketing data science a commercially urgent domain — yet no agentic benchmark exists for it.

### How does this differ from standard approaches?

Standard agentic evaluations treat the agent+LLM as an atomic unit and report a single accuracy or pass-rate score. This work treats the **agent scaffold** and **base model** as orthogonal independent variables in a two-way ANOVA, enabling causal attribution of performance variance — a methodological contribution independent of the empirical results.

---

## Scope

A single paper reporting:
- A **3 × 3 × 6 × 5** evaluation matrix (3 agents × 3 base models × 6 tasks × 5 seeds)
- A **lifecycle × autonomy annotation rubric** applied to all 54 agent-task cells (7 stages × 5 levels)
- Two pre-registered hypothesis tests (H1: model variance dominance; H2: autonomy-ceiling asymmetry)
- A **paper skeleton** (Introduction, Related Work, Methodology, Limitations) with Results/Discussion as author-filled placeholders

**Out of scope:** proprietary integrated-stack agents, new agent architectures, new base models, human-in-the-loop studies, cost/latency optimization.
